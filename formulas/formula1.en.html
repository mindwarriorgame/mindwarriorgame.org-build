Illusion says that the irritants are outside and must be changed.

But in fact they are inside. Imagine you are on your deathbed. Would you want to come back to today? Would you be happy? Then why aren’t you happy now, when you are already in this situation!

The source of suffering is not external; it is illusion (which convinces you of the opposite)!

Illusion doesn’t remind you how lucky you are to be born in the 21st century, to have access to clean water, the internet, shops, medicine (like painkillers at the dentist).

In reality, the source of suffering is inside. It is illusion. Let it go; stop seeing the source outside!

Illusion says that the past and future exist.

In reality, the past is gone, the future does not exist, and when it comes it immediately becomes the past — that is, it disappears.

The illusory past and future are the “second arrow”. Shift your attention to your sensations in the present moment. Is it really so painful and unbearable that it deserves this level of suffering?

The roots of your suffering are in a past that does not exist and a future that does not exist.

In reality there is only “now”; throw away past and future, even the very next moment! If you are not “here and now”, you are imprisoned by illusion. Reset the past. Are you really so bad off right now? Are you really missing something in your present moment?

There is reality, and there is illusion.

Actions taken from illusion are random and lead to suffering. (The premise is false.)

Actions aimed at improving illusion are meaningless, because illusion does not exist in reality. It will dissolve together with your personality.

Actions aimed at reducing the suffering of all living beings are your imprint in eternity. Karma does not disappear. This is creating the conditions for a better world.

You can suffer, or you can not suffer. Illusion will disappear, and reality doesn’t care. Only actions and consequences matter. Illusion will dissolve, but the consequences will remain. It is foolish to use illusion as the basis for your thoughts and actions.

Reality will win anyway; drop the illusion! Don’t think of it as something valuable.

Illusion can be useful, but there is a big difference between treating it as a parable (like one of Aesop’s fables) and treating it as objective reality.

Why believe the rambling stories invented by a schizophrenic? Why believe the illusion that devalues everything?
